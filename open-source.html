Introduction

Log4Shell

In late 2021, the Log4J zero-day vulnerability, Log4Shell was revealed. Effecting countless programs, Log4Shell allowed hackers to execute malicious code remotely. The disastrous effects of Log4Shell reveals the disastrous effects of issues in dependencies.

Why support matters

According to Eric Raymond's essay The Cathedral and the Bazaar, Linus's law states: “ given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow. ” More support means more bugs such as Log4Shell can be found and patched quicker or detected before entering a release. This is also important because of how much code is outdated and can contain vulnrabilities.

Google's Open Source Maintenance Crew

Due to Log4Shell and other similar vulnerabilities along with a push from the White House to improve software security, Google recently created its Open Source Maintenance Crew which attempts to protect the security of upstream projects and critical open source dependencies.

This is not enough

Despite gaining more support from big corporations, smaller organizations must also do their part to help fix issues in the open source projects they utilize. Given open source codeâ€™s massive economic benefits and usage, more returned support is expected.

Solutions

Promoting GPL/LGPL

Due to the popularly used MIT and Apache licenses, companies which use an open source product are not required to give back contributions to the code. If more projects used GPL or LGPL this problem would naturally fix itself. This is not feasible however, as many projects do not want to or can not change to a copyleft license.

Government intervention

Although the government has acted to aid this issue, these are not enough and too slow. The government ought to instead provide incentives for open source developers and companies which contribute back to open source projects.
